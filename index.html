⚠️ Read the map with these caveats
  • Two different markets. Demand is domestic university applications; supply is the international-student CRICOS register. They are related proxies for a field’s momentum but not the same market — read the map directionally, never as a literal demand-over-supply ratio.
  • All-levels CRICOS is VET-dominated. The all-levels count is dominated by VET / private-provider churn, so the higher-education lens (Bachelor and above) is the university read. The two can diverge sharply — e.g. Architecture & Building: +246 all-levels but −14 at degree level.
  • Different time bases. Supply is one-year momentum (a 2025→2026 snapshot pair); demand is a multi-year share trend. And demand is a share of applications — it can rise even as total applications fall.
  • Join key. Both sources are joined on the ASCED broad field of education — native to each, so no crosswalk is needed, but it is coarse (10 fields).
  • ACU figure is domestic commencing enrolments by field — a portfolio-presence proxy, not a full load or revenue measure.
  • 4-digit sub-fields are Health & Agriculture only. The DoE demand source publishes application data at 4-digit for just these two broad fields, so the granularity drill-down is limited to them. At 4-digit there is no ACU overlay — ACU enrolments are broad-field only, so sub-field bubbles are uniform.
